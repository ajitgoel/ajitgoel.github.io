Personalized Health Recommendations: Scaling Real-Time Intelligence

April 16, 2026 • 10 min read • Safeway Case Study

When I joined Safeway, one of the first big challenges was handling 1.8 million users who all wanted something different. We weren't just looking at what they bought last week; we were trying to index their chronic conditions, nutrition goals, and dietary needs into real-time recommendations. No one wants to wait 5 seconds for a page to load just to see a recipe, so the performance bar was high.

9TB Data Processed
1.8M Active Users
<200ms Latency

The Problem

Scaling personalized health advice isn't easy. You have to mash together health profiles (like diabetes or heart health) with years of grocery purchase history. My team and I needed a solution that was:

  • Fast: Recommendations had to be ready the second someone logged in.
  • Accurate: Medical and dietary rules are strict. If someone is vegan, you can't show them steak recipes.
  • Reliable: If the recommendation engine goes down, the whole experience breaks.

How We Built It

We looked at a few options. We tried nightly batch jobs, but they were too slow—by the time the job finished, the data was old. We also looked at Google's BigQuery Health Data Layer, but it didn't play nice with our existing legacy stack.

In the end, we decided to build a real-time orchestration engine. It works by capturing the user's ID at login and immediately pulling data from three main sources: their health profile, their shopping history, and our SpEL-based rule engine.

Result

The shift to real-time was a game changer. We're now processing 9 terabytes of data with sub-200ms latency. The best part? The system actually learns. As users interact with their recommendations, the ranking improves. This project wasn't just about moving data—it was about making it useful.
