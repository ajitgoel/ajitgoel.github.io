Personalized Health Recommendations: Scaling Real-Time Intelligence

April 16, 2026 • 10 min read • Safeway Case Study

In the modern retail landscape, "one size fits all" is a relic of the past. At Safeway, we faced a formidable challenge: how to deliver hyper-personalized product and recipe recommendations that not only reflect a user’s purchase history but also their unique health profiles—all while maintaining the performance expected of a tier-1 digital experience.

9TB Data Processed
1.8M Active Users
<200ms Latency

The Challenge

We needed a system capable of synthesizing vast amounts of data—chronic conditions, dietary preferences, nutrition goals, and years of household purchase history—into actionable recommendations. The solution had to be:

  • Scalable: Support millions of concurrent users without degradation.
  • Flexible: Allow for rapid adjustments to ranking logic and business rules.
  • Fast: Recommendations needed to be ready the moment a user logged in.

The Architectural Crossroads

We evaluated three primary approaches to solve this data orchestration problem:

  1. Nightly Batch Generation: Standard batch processing but struggled with data freshness and the sheer volume of permutations.
  2. BigQuery HDL (Google Health Data Layer): Faced significant technical barriers in integrating with existing legacy systems.
  3. Real-time Generation: Dynamically assembling recommendations at request time.

The Decision

Traditional nightly batch processing simply couldn't meet our performance needs for 1.8 million users. Real-time generation emerged as the only solution that could provide the responsiveness and scalability required for a modern health application.

The Solution: Real-Time Orchestration

Upon user login, the system captures key identifiers and triggers a real-time recommendation engine. This engine integrates:

  • User Health Profiles: Chronic conditions (e.g., diabetes, heart health), dietary preferences (vegan, keto), and specific nutrition goals.
  • Household Purchase History: Analyzing patterns to ensure recommendations are relevant to actual buying habits.

The system dynamically assembles and ranks recommendations for each widget, ensuring a unique and relevant experience for every user. We implemented advanced caching layers and configuration management to ensure speed and flexibility without sacrificing accuracy.

Outcome and Impact

By shifting to a real-time model, we successfully launched a highly responsive personalization platform. We now serve 9 terabytes of data to over 1.8 million users, delivering a personalized experience that evolves with the user.

This architecture doesn't just solve today's challenge—it provides the foundation for future innovations in AI-driven health and wellness retail.
